⚠️ Safety Rules

Before beginning any project, please ensure you follow our strict safety guidelines:

  • Always use thermal tools with supervision.
  • Work in a highly ventilated area to avoid inhaling plastic fumes if melting materials.
  • Wear heat-resistant gloves and safety goggles when operating heat sources.

Project 1: How to Make Plastic Beads

A step-by-step guide to safely heat and shape PET plastic into beads.

Tools Needed:

  • Clean PET bottles
  • Heat gun or supervised oven
  • Scissors & sandpaper
  • Metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. Cut the PET bottles into thin, long strips.
  2. Wrap the strips tightly around a metal skewer.
  3. Apply heat evenly using a heat gun until the plastic shrinks and fuses.
  4. Let cool completely before removing from the skewer.

Project 2: Woven Plastic Coasters

Learn how to turn plastic grocery bags into durable yarn (plarn) and weave them into colorful table coasters.

Tools Needed:

  • 6–10 Clean plastic grocery bags (different colors make great patterns)
  • Sharp scissors
  • A sturdy piece of cardboard (approx. 5x5 inches)
  • A ruler and a pen
  • A large tapestry needle or a small safety pin

Step 1: Create the "Plarn" (Plastic Yarn)

  1. Flatten & Prep: Lay a plastic bag completely flat on your work surface. Smooth out all the wrinkles.
  2. Cut Off Edges: Use your scissors to cut off the top handles and the bottom seam of the bag, leaving you with a clean, rectangular plastic tube.
  3. Fold & Slice: Fold the tube lengthwise a few times, then cut across it horizontally at 1-inch intervals to create several loops.
  4. Loop-to-Loop Knot: Take two loops and interlock them by pulling one loop through the other (a lark's head knot). Pull gently to secure. Repeat this step with all your loops to create a single continuous strand of plarn, then roll it into a ball.

Step 2: Build Your Cardboard Loom

  1. Mark the Edges: Take your 5x5 inch piece of cardboard. Using your ruler, make a mark every 1/2 inch along the top edge and the bottom edge.
  2. Cut Notches: Cut 1/4-inch deep slits into every mark you just made. Ensure the top and bottom notches line up perfectly.

Step 3: Warp the Loom

  1. Secure the Base: Wedge the end of a plarn strand into the top-left notch, leaving a 2-inch tail on the back of the cardboard.
  2. Create Verticals: Bring the strand down to the bottom-left notch and slide it in. Guide it behind the cardboard tab to the next bottom notch, pull it back up to the corresponding top notch, and wedge it in.
  3. Complete the Warp: Repeat this up-and-down pattern until all notches are filled. These vertical lines are called your warp strands. Cut the plarn and leave a tail on the back.

Step 4: Weave the Coaster

  1. Over and Under: Thread a long strand of a different colored plarn into your tapestry needle (or tie it to a safety pin). Start at the bottom row and pass the needle *over* the first vertical warp strand, *under* the second, *over* the third, and so on until you reach the opposite side.
  2. Return Row: For the next row, reverse the pattern. If you went *over* a strand in the previous row, go *under* it this time.
  3. Pack It Tight: After completing each row, use your fingers or a household fork to gently press the woven plarn down toward the bottom so your weave becomes dense and sturdy.
  4. Adding New Colors: When you run out of plarn or want to swap colors, simply knot the end of your old strand to a new strand of plarn and keep weaving. Hide the knot inside the weave layers.

Step 5: Off the Loom & Finishing

  1. Remove the Loops: Once your weaving reaches the very top and is tightly packed, flip the loom over. Gently slide the loops off the cardboard notches.
  2. Tie Off Tails: Tie adjacent warp loops together in pairs using simple double knots along the edges to keep your coaster from unraveling.
  3. Trim: Cut away any excess dangling plarn tails from the back or sides to give your coaster a clean, professional finish.
